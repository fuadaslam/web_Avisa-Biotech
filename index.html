DHA
  • DHA is an Omega 3 fatty acid which constitutes the primary structural component of skin, cerebral cortex, brain and retina.
  • It is a well-recognized dietary supplement worldwide
  • It can be synthesized from α- linolenic acid
  • It is directly obtained from maternal milk, fish oil or algal sources.
  • As the body doesn’t produce sufficient amount of DHA, it should be supplemented through diet.
INNOVATIONS AND UNIQUENESS
  • Novel process for production of DHA omega 3 phospholipid molecule
  • All algal DHA producers have Triglyceride or Ester form currently
  • IWe are the only producer of water soluble Phospholipid form (Water Soluble )
  • Bioavailability of phospholipids is more by 40 %
  • Numerous benefits only in Phospholipid form.
  • More effective and efficient form of DHA

ARA
  • ARA (C20H32O2) is an essential omega-6 fatty acid 20:4(ω-6)
  • ARA plays vital role in immunity, blood clotting, brain development in infants
  • ARA is not manufactured in the body and need to be taken as dietary supplements.
  • Sources: poultry, animal organs, meat, fish, seafood, eggs, nuts, fatty seeds, derived vegetable oils.
  • Deficiencies: abnormalities in the skin, nervous system, immune and inflammatory systems, cardiovascular system, endocrine system, kidneys, respiratory and reproductive systems
Eicosapentaenoic Acid (EPA) Omega -3
  • EPA ( Omega 3 ) along with DHA can lower the risk of cardiovascular diseases
  • Natural Fish oil source generally have a EPA to DHA ratio of 3:2 (180 mg EPA plus 120 mg DHA)
  • Rising veganism and vegetarianism and protection against cardiovascular has increased the demand for vegan non fish oil based EPA
  • Our priority is to produce from a specific microorganism which naturally produce DHA to EPA ratio in 2: 1 ratio and separate out the EPA using downstream processing .
